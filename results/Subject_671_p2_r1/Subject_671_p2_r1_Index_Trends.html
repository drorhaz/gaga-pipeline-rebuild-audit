Subject 671_p2_r1: Index Trend Plots

Longitudinal shifts in behavioral strategy (T1 → T2 → T3). Dynamics (blue), Pose (orange), Reach (green).

Volume

Volume: 3D convex hull of the (PC1, PC2, PC3) cloud per session. Larger volume in T2 indicates broader spatial exploration in the principal-component space.

Shannon Entropy

Shannon Entropy: Information density of the joint-variance distribution. Higher entropy in T2 suggests the motor system is exploring more diverse, less predictable joint combinations.

Gini Coefficient

Gini Coefficient: Reconstructs session variance from the PCA weight matrix. A low Gini in T2 indicates a shift from few dominant joints to more democratic, whole-body integration.

Sparseness

Sparseness (System Activation): The percentage of joints with <1% contribution to variance. Lower sparseness in T2 indicates that more of the body's degrees of freedom have been activated by the intervention.

Axial–Peripheral Ratio

Axial–Peripheral Ratio: Central (Spine/Neck/Hips) vs distal (Hands/Feet) variance loadings. An increase in T2 suggests a shift toward core-driven, embodied movement.
